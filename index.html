The natural environment of Japan creates a rich culture of food and drink in the country. The island nation has a lot of green space, which gives its food a distinct flavor from the produce in other countries. The country also has abundant water sources, which contributes to great seafood dishes. Fish from the ocean is popular in every region of Japan and is often served sashimi-style with rice or inside somen noodles. Vegetables are abundant in the country, so they feature prominently in Japanese cuisine as well as local dishes such as ramen or gyoza dumplings. Popular vegetable dishes include miso soup, tempura. Additionally, sweet dishes made with fresh fruits also incorporate native ingredients into their taste profiles.
